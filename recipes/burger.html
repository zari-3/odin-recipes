Burger

Description

A cheeseburger is a hamburger with melted cheese on top of the meat patty, added near the end of the cooking time. Cheeseburgers can include variations in structure, ingredients and composition.

From the best beef to the best bun to the best cheese, today’s burger recipe also comes with your choice of burger sauces: a tomato one or my quick creamy Special Burger Sauce.

Ingredients

  • Ground Beef: 450 g (1 lb) ground chuck.
  • Cheese: 4 slices of cheese.
  • Butter: 2 tablespoons, softened.
  • kosher salt: 1 teaspoon.
  • coarse black pepper: 1/2 teaspoon.
  • Oil: 1 tablespoon high-smoke point oil.
  • Vegetables: Crisp iceberg lettuce, sliced ripe tomatoes, and thin red onion rings.
  • Pickles: Dill pickle chips.
  • Sauce: Ketchup, yellow mustard, and mayonnaise.

Steps

  1. Toast the Buns: Split the burger buns open and spread a thin layer of softened butter on the cut sides. Heat a large skillet or griddle over medium heat. Place the buns cut-side down in the pan and toast for 1 to 2 minutes until they turn golden brown and crispy. Set them aside.
  2. Shape the Beef Patties: Divide the ground beef evenly into 4 equal portions (112 g each). Gently shape each portion into a round ball, then press it down into a flat patty about 1/2 inch thick. Make the patties slightly wider than your burger buns, as they will shrink during cooking. Press a shallow indentation with your thumb into the center of each patty to prevent it from puffing up.
  3. Season Generously: Just before cooking, season both sides of the beef patties generously with the kosher salt and black pepper. Do not mix the salt into the raw meat beforehand, as this can make the texture tough.
  4. Sear the Patties: Turn the heat up to medium-high and add the vegetable oil to the skillet. Once the pan is smoking hot, carefully place the patties inside. Sear them without moving them for 3 minutes until a deeply browned, crispy crust forms on the bottom.
  5. Flip and Melt Cheese: Flip the patties over using a sturdy spatula. Immediately place one slice of cheese on top of each cooked side. Cook for another 2 minutes. To help the cheese melt completely, you can pour a teaspoon of water into the pan and cover it with a lid for 30 seconds to trap the steam.
  6. Assemble the Cheeseburger: Spread your desired condiments (like mayonnaise, ketchup, or mustard) onto the toasted bottom bun. Lay down the lettuce, tomato slices, and pickles. Place the hot cheesy patty directly on top, cover it with the top bun, and serve immediately while hot.
