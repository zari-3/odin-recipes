Fried chicken

Description

Fried chicken is a beloved culinary staple featuring chicken pieces coated in seasoned flour or batter and cooked to a crispy golden brown. You can enjoy it at local restaurants, buy ready-to-cook packs online, or make it at home using a variety of marinades and dredging techniques.

Discover the technique for making deliciously crispy fried chicken with a crunchy coating on the outside and juicy, tender chicken inside.

Ingredients

  • The Meat: 1 kg chicken pieces (drumsticks, thighs, or wings)
  • The Marinade: 500 ml buttermilk, 1 tsp salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper, 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
  • The Breading: 250g all-purpose flour (maida), 60g cornstarch (cornflour)
  • The Spice Mix: 1 tbsp salt, 1 tbsp garlic powder, 1 tbsp onion powder, 1 tbsp paprika, 1 tsp black pepper, 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
  • The Frying: 1 liter neutral cooking oil (like sunflower, peanut, or vegetable oil)

Steps

  1. Marinate the Chicken: Whisk the buttermilk, salt, black pepper, and cayenne pepper together in a large bowl. Submerge the chicken pieces fully in the liquid. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for the juiciest results.
  2. Prepare the Flour Coating: Whisk the all-purpose flour, cornstarch, and the entire spice mix together in a large, shallow dish or baking pan. The cornstarch is the secret to getting a lighter, crunchier crust.
  3. Coat the Chicken: Remove a piece of chicken from the buttermilk, letting the excess drip off. Dredge it thoroughly in the flour mixture, pressing down firmly so the flour sticks to every crevice. For an extra-crunchy crust, dip the floured chicken back into the buttermilk quickly, then coat it in the flour a second time.
  4. Fry to Golden Perfection: Heat your cooking oil in a deep pan or Dutch oven to 175°C. Carefully lower 3 to 4 chicken pieces into the hot oil without overcrowding the pan. Fry thighs and drumsticks for 12-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the crust is deeply golden brown and the internal temperature reaches 75°C.
  5. Drain and Rest: Remove the chicken from the oil using tongs or a slotted spoon. Rest the pieces on a wire cooling rack placed over a baking sheet for 5 minutes. Avoid draining them on paper towels, as this traps steam and makes the bottom crust soggy.
